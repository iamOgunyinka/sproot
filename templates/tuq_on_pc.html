The Way

TuqOnThePC is used exclusively by Tuq Android App for uploading Q&A to the web. It is as simple as it sounds.

  • Register as an administrator here.
  • Download and install the software here.
  • On first install, it asks you for an endpoint, when prompted, enter {{main_link}}, choose "OK" and wait a couple seconds while your software is setup.
  • If your account has been activated, you may login by selecting Menu >> Login.
  • After a successful login, you can perform other administrative tasks.

What is a repository?

Think of this as a folder that house files, as many files as you can think. OR. Think of it as a Class of Computer Science in a 2015/2016 academic session, you then have a number of courses taken that session. That academic session is a repository/folder and the courses taken are the files in that folder.

How do I upload a question script?

These are the basic steps to take:

  • Select Create new course from the Questions menu.
  • You're presented with a window that has two tabs, fill in the columns in the "Settings" tab.
  • Add each questions from the List of questions tab by clicking on "Add new"
  • On the "Add new" question window, the field that says "Question" is where you'll ask your question like:
    What part is labelled 'A' in the image below?

  • The options field are multiple choice options, type each options and select "Add". The minimum is 3 and the maximum is 4.
  • When you wish to change an option, simply double click on it, Clear the field and select "OK" to delete that option.
  • Simply double click on it, change the field to whatever you want and select "OK" to update that option.
  • Choose the correct option in the "Answer" drop down menu, so that the server can help identify the right option during grading.
  • To use an image in a specific question, select the "Use image" option( 100KB max. ) or "Use text file" to upload a TXT file.
  • Repeat this option until you're done.
  • Upload immediately.

How do I get a link to my questions?

From the menu page, scroll to "Repositories" and then choose "List my repositories". It will list all the links to your repository.
