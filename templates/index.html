Welcome to the official website of Tuq

Tuq, pronounced Tuck, is an acronym for The Universal Quiz App(without the A, of course). It is a Q&A platform for quiz lovers, universities/colleges or high schools, employers, and the general public. Right now, it is restricted to multiple choice questions that requires no explicit input texts.

How does the Android app work?

Conventionally, all the standalone quiz mobile apps I've seen on the web embed questions and their answers in the application binary(APK). Thus, a tiny mistake in the Q&A gets carried over until the next application update. That means, the general public don't get a proper Q&A update unless they update their app to call the shots, ONLY the developer does. Moreover, most of these apps are single-focused for a domain -- an app for English language IS for English, a medical Q&A app is strictly for that.

How about an Android app that doesn't really have a single Q&A embedded in it but depends solely on the general public to set questions and their respective answers? This app is dependent on data sent to and from a server. On its own, it doesn't do much, other than to display questions and send your data to the server for grading, so you can view your results after some minutes, say, 2 minutes on the same app. To do this, there is a Desktop/PC software for Q&A administrators that are used to upload questions to a server. When questions are uploaded on to the server, it generates a unique web link, this unique link points to at most one repository. This link is what your administrator will give out to you. Each link contains detailed information on every question that is within its repository.

All you need is to get a repository link from your administrator, your friends and/or an employer, then click on the + button on the Repository page, enter the link and wait for Tuq to do its magic. Each valid links are saved under the Saved Links page for use in the future. Sometimes, submitting a quiz for grading may fail, most likely due to 3 reasons:

  • You have already taken that quiz
  • Your network
  • Server problem

In order not to lose your precious data and let you down, the "Submission Error" page list all the quizzes that have been taken but failed to be submitted. You can then manually resend your "scripts" for grading.
